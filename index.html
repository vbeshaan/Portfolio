SeaGuardian Drone

Semi-Autonomous Lifeguard Rescue Drone System

Illinois Design Challenge Competition 2-Day CAD Competition Entry Conceptual Design Only - Not Physically Built
Design Competition Entry: This project was created for the Illinois Design Challenge, a competitive 2-day CAD modeling competition. The concept was fully designed and CAD modeled with comprehensive implementation plans, but was not physically manufactured or built.

Innovative semi-autonomous lifeguard rescue drone featuring AI-powered drowning detection and precision life preserver deployment system. This comprehensive design demonstrates advanced integration of thermal imaging, autonomous flight control, and emergency response systems.

Design Competition Achievements:

  • Complete CAD modeling and system architecture in 2-day timeframe
  • AI-powered drowning detection using thermal imaging technology
  • VTOL capability design for rapid emergency deployment
  • Autonomous life preserver deployment with 1-3 feet accuracy targeting
  • Comprehensive implementation strategy and technical specifications
  • Modular design approach for multiple emergency applications

Physical Design Specifications (CAD Modeled):

  • Propulsion: 11-inch carbon fiber propellers with VTOL configuration
  • Structure: Waterproof stainless steel and ABS plastic body
  • Power System: Multiple Sony Murata VTC5A 18650 batteries (2600mAh 25A)
  • Detection: FLIR DUO PRO R thermal camera with AI computer vision
  • Communication: Gizont Tactical Gooseneck Lora Antenna for long-range operation
  • Navigation: GPS receiver, accelerometer, gyroscope for stable flight
  • Computing: Waterproof carbon fiber casing with microprocessor and I/O
  • Safety: Regulatory-compliant strobe and warning lights
  • Payload: Servo-controlled droppers with independent latches on rigid tail
  • Life Preservers: Collapsible rubber and nylon with high-density weights

Implementation Strategy (Conceptual):

Deployment Protocol

Station at lifeguard posts or vessels with VTOL capability for immediate response to emergency situations.

Operational Parameters

Passive patrol at 150-200 feet altitude with continuous thermal scanning and pattern recognition.

AI Detection System

Advanced thermal imaging algorithms trained on drowning behavior patterns for real-time water surface analysis.

Emergency Response

Autonomous descent to 10-30 feet altitude for precise life preserver deployment within 1-3 feet accuracy.

Control Systems

Manual RC override capabilities with autonomous AI deployment and machine learning optimization.

Recovery Protocol

"Back to Home" feature for automatic return, battery management, and payload refitting cycles.
